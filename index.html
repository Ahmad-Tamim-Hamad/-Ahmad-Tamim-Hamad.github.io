about me

I am Ahmad Tamim Hamad, a Ph.D. student in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at the University of Missouri, with an expected graduation in 2025. My academic journey includes a Master’s degree in Information Technology from the University of Mysore, India, and a Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science from Gharjistan University, Afghanistan. With over three years of experience in software engineering, data science, and deep learning technologies, I have developed a robust foundation in both theoretical knowledge and practical application. Currently, I am working in the Scalable Data Science (SDS) lab, focusing on cutting-edge research in scalable applications and deep learning.

My roles as a Graduate Research Assistant and Software Engineer have equipped me with the skills to tackle complex problems and contribute to innovative solutions. Beyond my professional pursuits, I have been actively involved in extracurricular activities, serving as the President of the Afghan Students Association at the University of Missouri-Columbia (2022-2023) and being an active member of Upsilon Pi Epsilon, the international honor society for computing and information disciplines.

I am passionate about lifelong learning and collaboration, and I am eager to join dynamic teams where I can continue to grow and contribute to meaningful advancements in technology. My goal is to leverage my expertise to drive impactful outcomes and make a significant difference in the world.
